Largest city and Capital city in British Columbia

The largest city in British Columbia is Vancouver, British Columbia and the capital city of British Columbia is Victoria, British Columbia. First, Vancouver is the city that has the largest population in British Columbia and is the 3rd largest metropolitan area in Canada. It is located in the southwestern portion of British Columbia to the east of Vancouver Island. West Vancouver borders Vancouver to the North, Burnaby borders Vancouver to the East, the Straight of Georgia borders Vancouver to the West, and Richmond borders Vancouver to the South. The current mayor of Vancouver is Gregor Robertson. Second, Victoria is a city that has the second largest population in British Columbia and is the 15th largest metropolitan area in Canada. It is located in the southern part of Vancouver Island and is about 100 km away from Seattle in The United States and Vancouver, British Columbia through plane or ferry. The current mayor of Victoria is Lisa Helps. Vancouver's tourist attractions include Stanley Park, Science World, and Vancouver Aquarium while Victoria's tourist attractions include Beacon Hill Park, Butchart Gardens, and Royal British Columbia Museum.

Professional sports teams in British Columbia

The professional sports teams in British Columbia include the Vancouver Canucks of the National Hockey League (NHL), Vancouver Whitecaps FC of Major League Soccer (MLS), and the BC Lions of the Canadian Football League (CFL).

Interesting facts about British Columbia

1. Prince Rupert, British Columbia is named as the wettest city in Canada as it averages nearly 2600 milimeters of rain per year.

2. Half or 50% of the British Columbian population is located in the region of Metro Vancouver.

3. Whistler, British Columbia holds the record for the longest non supported cable car in the world.

4. British Columbia is the province with the warmest climate in Canada.

5. The sport of Bathub racing began in Nanaimo, British Columbia
